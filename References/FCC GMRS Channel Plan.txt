==============================================================================
FCC GMRS CHANNEL PLAN  (General Mobile Radio Service)
==============================================================================
Source: FCC rules, 47 CFR Part 95 Subpart E (GMRS) - §95.563 (frequencies),
        §95.567 (power / authorized bandwidth). Captured 2026-07-01.
Purpose: definitive source-of-record for the GMRS channels in
         `CHIRP Lists/GMRS and MURS.csv` (simplex ch 1-22 + the 8 generic
         repeater channels).

GMRS uses 30 channels total: 22 simplex/FRS-shared channels plus 8 repeater
pairs (the repeater OUTPUTs are the same 8 "462 main" frequencies as ch 15-22).

Repeater channels  -  467 MHz main (repeater INPUTs, +5 MHz offset)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A GMRS repeater listens on 462.xxx (the ch 15-22 output) and receives on the
467.xxx input 5 MHz above it. Program a repeater channel as: Frequency = the
462 output, Duplex = "+", Offset = 5.000000.

Output (RX, MHz)   Input (TX, MHz)   Named in baseline
462.5500           467.5500          GMRS15R
462.5750           467.5750          GMRS16R
462.6000           467.6000          GMRS17R
462.6250           467.6250          GMRS18R
462.6500           467.6500          GMRS19R

462.7000           467.7000          GMRS21R
462.7250           467.7250          GMRS22R
Repeater inputs (467 main): max power 50 W, 20 kHz bandwidth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The 141.3 Hz "travel tone" (convention, NOT an FCC rule)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
141.3 Hz has become the de facto CTCSS "travel tone" for open GMRS repeaters:
a traveler can switch to a repeater channel with 141.3 and try to key up without
knowing the local tone. It is a widely-adopted convention, not standardized or
required - each repeater owner picks their own tone, and 141.3 is simply the
most commonly encountered / agreed travel-and-emergency tone. 462.675 (ch 20)
+ 141.3 is the recognized national GMRS calling/travel channel (hence GMRS20R*).
The generic repeater block in `GMRS and MURS.csv` (GMRS15R-22R) programs all 8
repeater outputs with TSQL 141.3 so any open repeater on the travel tone can be
worked/heard while mobile. Real local repeaters use their actual tones - see
`Local GMRS Repeaters.txt` and `CHIRP Lists/local gmrs repeaters.csv`.

Sources for the travel-tone convention:
  - myGMRS.com forums, "Use of Travel Tone"
  - RadioReference.com, "The history behind 462.675 MHz and the travel tone"
  (Convention only; the frequency/offset facts above are the FCC regulatory source.)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Note on power in this repo
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The house convention in the Complete Radio Plans uses the radio's high setting
(`10W`) on the 462 GMRS channels. The FCC limits above (5 W on ch 1-7, 0.5 W on
ch 8-14) are the regulatory ceilings; ch 8-14 must stay NFM. Power is set per
radio - the owner adjusts as desired; these limits are recorded for reference.
