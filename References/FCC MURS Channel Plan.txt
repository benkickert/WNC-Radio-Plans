==============================================================================
FCC MURS CHANNEL PLAN  (Multi-Use Radio Service)
==============================================================================
Source: FCC rules, 47 CFR Part 95 Subpart J (MURS) - §95.2563 (frequencies),
        §95.2567 (power / authorized bandwidth). Captured 2026-07-01.
Purpose: definitive source-of-record for the MURS channels in
         `CHIRP Lists/GMRS and MURS.csv` (slots 81-85).

MURS is a 5-channel, license-free VHF service. Key limits (all channels):
  - Max power: 2 W ERP.  No repeaters, no interconnection to the phone network.
  - External / elevated antennas permitted (unlike FRS).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Ch   Frequency (MHz)   Max bandwidth        Mode      Common name
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1   151.8200          11.25 kHz (narrow)   NFM       "MURS 1"
 2   151.8800          11.25 kHz (narrow)   NFM       "MURS 2"
 3   151.9400          11.25 kHz (narrow)   NFM       "MURS 3"
 4   154.5700          20 kHz (wide ok)     FM        "Blue Dot"
 5   154.6000          20 kHz (wide ok)     FM        "Green Dot"

Notes:
  - The three 151 MHz channels (1-3) are restricted to 11.25 kHz bandwidth -
    program them NFM.
  - The two 154 MHz channels (4-5) allow up to 20 kHz - wideband FM is fine.
    154.570 = "blue dot", 154.600 = "green dot" (the old business-band dot
    colors that pre-date MURS; these two channels were grandfathered in).
  - MURS is simplex only: Duplex empty, Offset 0.000000.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Note on power in this repo
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The house convention in the Complete Radio Plans carries `10W` on the MURS rows
(the radio's high setting). The FCC MURS ceiling is 2 W ERP - well below that.
Power is set per radio; the owner adjusts as desired. Recorded here for
reference so a MURS-legal build can be trimmed to 2 W if wanted.
