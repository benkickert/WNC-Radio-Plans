SAR / INTEROP FREQUENCY REFERENCE
=================================
Verified: 2026-06-29
Purpose:  Source-of-record for the CMC search-and-rescue / interop CHIRP List
          (CHIRP Lists/CMC.csv). Everything in that list traces to an entry here.

"CMC" = CMC-SAR, a search-and-rescue crew (joint operations with HCSAR = Henderson
County SAR). Their channel set, names, and operating practice come from the CMC
Baofeng radio operations manual (see SOURCES).

SOURCES
-------
- CMC-SAR radio operations manual:
    References/Radio operations manual.docx.pdf
    (definitive source for the CMC channel set, names, and operating practice)

    https://www.cisa.gov/sites/default/files/2024-12/NIFOG%202.02_508%20FINAL%20VERSION%2012%2003%202024.pdf
- RadioReference, National Interoperability:
    https://www.radioreference.com/db/aid/7742

NOTES
-----
- Mode: the CMC manual says set the radio WIDE ("for general use"), so CMCSAR1-3 are
  FM. VSAR16 and UTAC41D are national interop channels and are narrowband (NFM) per
  the band plan.
- Tones: the CMC manual programs NO access tones (carrier squelch); CTCSS is set ad
  hoc only if another agency on the channel requires it. So all channels are carrier
  here. UTAC41D's NIFOG tone (156.7) is recorded above for if/when TX is needed.
- UTAC41D TX discipline: per the CMC manual, do NOT transmit unless in a joint
  CMC/HCSAR operation and directed — it is heavily used by local emergency agencies.
- The off-raster CMC frequencies (462.5600 / 462.6800 / 467.7025) are intentional:
  the manual states they were "carefully chosen so as not to interfere" with other
  users in the region.
- Name "VSAR16" is the CMC manual's own tag (the bare NIFOG tag is "VSAR").
