================================================================================
REPEATER SCORING — repeater-scores.app
================================================================================
Source: repeater-scores.app  |  Captured: 2026-06-25
Applies to: the scored multi-state repeater pools in CHIRP Lists/
            (e.g. GA-IL-IN-KY-NC-OH-SC-TN-VA.csv), whose Comment field carries
            a "STATE/<score><flags>" badge.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCORING EXPLAINED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Repeater scores are in the style of "high score", not a grade or rating out of 10.
EX: The highest scoring repeater is currently a 15.5 from KB3AWQ out of
Williamsport PA.

Scores add up from several signals, including regular nets, linked services like
AllStar, EchoLink, or IRLP, linked repeater systems, broader stated coverage,
wide-area operation, and public-service affiliations like ARES, RACES, or SKYWARN.

Coverage can matter a lot. A repeater with strong stated mobile or regional
coverage can score well even if it has few other flags.

Repeaters that are more isolated from nearby sites can also receive a bonus, since
they may be more valuable in sparse coverage areas.

Use a higher Score >= value when you want a tighter list of higher-confidence
repeaters. Use a lower threshold when you want a broader list or are traveling
through thinner coverage areas.

You can set a different minimum score for each state or province to keep dense
regions selective while leaving sparse regions more inclusive.

Data comes from RepeaterBook, so a lower score does not always mean a repeater is
bad. It may simply have less detail available.

If a file grows too large for the radio, the builder automatically raises score
thresholds until the export fits.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCORING EXAMPLES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A repeater with broad stated coverage and several weekly nets can score well even
if it has no linked-service flags.

A repeater with AllStar or EchoLink, linked repeaters, and public-service
affiliations will usually rise quickly because multiple useful signals stack
together.

A repeater with only one small clue, like a single net or a minimal description,
may stay lower even if it is still perfectly usable on the air.

An isolated repeater in a sparse area can pick up bonus value because it may fill a
coverage gap that nearby sites do not.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCORING SHORTHAND  (badge form: ST/SXCWN)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On the ICOM-5100, the Sub Name field uses a compact badge in the form ST/SXCWN to
display the repeater score and relevant details that likely matter to you.

ST  the state or province abbreviation. Example: OR/ means Oregon.
S   the repeater score shown as a single digit. Decimal scores are floored, and
    anything above 9 is shown as 9.
X   the link slot: S for a system-linked repeater, L for other linked repeaters,
    and - for neither.
C   the connectivity slot: A for AllStar, E for EchoLink, B for both, and - for
    neither.
W   the repeater looks wide-area, either because RepeaterBook marks it that way or
    because the stated coverage is strong enough to qualify.
N   the repeater has at least 2 nets. A single net does not light that flag.

A dash like - means that flag is not present for that repeater.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SCORING + DATA SHORTHAND EXAMPLES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
OR/7SEWN  Oregon, score 7, system-linked, has EchoLink, qualifies as wide-area,
          and has at least two nets.
OR/4-BW-  Oregon, score 4, has both AllStar and EchoLink and qualifies as
          wide-area, but is not marked linked and does not have enough nets to
          light the N flag.
WA/4L-W-  Washington, score 4, linked and wide-area, but has neither AllStar nor
          EchoLink, and does not have enough nets to light the N flag.
WA/1----  Washington, score 1 and none of the tracked badge flags present.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
NOTE ON THIS REPO'S CSV COMMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The single-digit / max-9 score rule above is specifically the ICOM-5100 Sub Name
*badge*. The scored CSV(s) in CHIRP Lists/ keep the FULL numeric score in the
Comment field (e.g. "GA/12SBWN" = score 12), then the same XCWN flags. So in those
files the number can exceed 9; the four flag characters decode exactly as above.
