============================================
Verified: 2026-06-29
Purpose:  Source-of-record for CHIRP Lists/Simplex - 2m and 70cm.csv.
          Every simplex channel in that list is a SERA-coordinated FM voice
          simplex frequency listed below.

SOURCE
------
SERA (Southeastern Repeater Association) Frequency Utilization Plans, in

  - sera-fup-440.pdf   (70 cm / 420-450 MHz)


2 m — FM VOICE SIMPLEX  (FUP-144)
---------------------------------
SERA 2 m FM voice simplex sub-bands: 146.400-146.585 and 147.405-147.585.
Channels marked "*" are permitted only if they don't interfere with nearby
repeater inputs; the owner's list uses ONLY non-* (clean) channels.

  National 2 m FM simplex calling channel: 146.520 (included in the list at
  slot 101 as "2mCall" - see NOTES).

  Non-* (clean) simplex channels:
    146.535  146.550  146.565  146.580  146.595
    147.510  147.525  147.540  147.555  147.570  147.585

  In the CSV (10 simplex channels at slots 102-111, plus 146.520 calling at 101):
    146.535 / .550 / .565 / .580 / .595
    147.525 / .540 / .555 / .570 / .585
  (147.510 deliberately skipped — used as an output by a distant Rockingham NC
   repeater.)

NOTES
-----
- The two national FM simplex CALLING channels are included at the top of the
  list (slots 100/101) so they carry the same slot numbers as in local baseline.csv:
    slot 100 = 70cmCall = 446.000  (national 70 cm FM simplex calling; SERA FUP-440)
    slot 101 = 2mCall   = 146.520  (national 2 m FM simplex calling;  SERA FUP-144)
  These are the standard national calling frequencies; every other row is an
  additional (non-calling) simplex channel. Calling rows use 10W (primary use);
  the additional simplex rows use 5.0W (bump per radio).
- All channels FM, carrier squelch (no tone). Power per radio.
- Two rows carry an extra club-simplex annotation from the LCARA reference
  (References/Lake Cumberland ARC Repeaters (Somerset KY).txt):
    146.580 = Somerset KY (LCARA) club simplex
    147.570 = Monticello KY (LCARA) club simplex
- Selection rule for extending the list: non-* SERA FM voice simplex only;
  cross-check any new channel against all local repeater CSVs for conflicts.
